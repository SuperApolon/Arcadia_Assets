While the three of them sat around the hearth chatting happily, a sudden shrill shout erupted from outside the guild.

Without so much as a greeting, two figures burst through the door at tremendous speed. The moment they entered the room, they began jumping up and down dramatically, flailing their entire bodies as if throwing a tantrum—though the outburst seemed directed at no one in particular.

“This is utterly ridicuLOUS, deshi! Fighting over nothing more than a piece of raw meat—what absolute idiocy, deshi! Truly foolish, deshi! If I could, I’d tie a Naumann elephant’s droppings to them and sink them straight into the River Styx, deshi!”

The owner of the strange speech pattern appeared backward into the guild hall as they complained.

It was a small creature—short in stature, covered in thick fur, and… talking.

Behind it came a young man with black hair and round glasses, watching over the creature with a weary expression.

“Please don’t make such a scene,” the bespectacled youth said calmly. “You’ll only exhaust yourself even more.”

In response, the strange creature answered his request by driving a headbutt straight into the young man’s groin.

The bespectacled youth collapsed to the ground in silent agony.

Leaving him behind, the creature nodded in apparent satisfaction and turned around.

Only then did its eyes meet those of the three adventurers sitting by the hearth.

“What are you lot staring at, deshi?”

Under the bizarre pressure radiating from the tiny being, the trio found themselves momentarily speechless.

Rabbit-like ears. Catlike whiskers. Standing upright on two legs.

The creature stared them down.

“Um… we came for the beginner tutorial…”

Swift answered cautiously.

Suddenly, the creature shouted again.

“Rubens isn’t here, deshi! This is why that idiot can’t be trusted, deshi! Leaving guests unattended—where did he run off to, deshi?! Someone bring me that Naumann elephant thing! Today I’ll finally sink him into the river, deshi!”

The three could only stare in confusion, unsure how to deal with the furious little creature.

At last, the bespectacled youth—apparently recovered from the headbutt—slowly rose from the floor.

“You know… getting angry like that in front of adventurers isn’t exactly ideal either.”

“Watch how you speak, deshi, you glasses maniac.”

When the creature’s head dipped slightly forward again, the three adventurers instinctively fell silent—fully aware of where the next headbutt might land.

“So then—what was it, deshi? Beginner tutorial, right? Good. We’ll begin immediately, deshi.”

The small creature puffed out its chest proudly.

“I should introduce myself properly, deshi! I am the guild master here—Cricket, deshi!”

With a small hop, the creature calling itself Cricket bounced over and sat down beside Eltz.

“What are you standing around for, Koza? Hurry and serve tea to our guests, deshi!”

The young man called Koza glared resentfully at Cricket, then staggered to his feet.

Muttering under his breath, he disappeared toward the back of the hut.

“Well then, we’ll begin by explaining how to use your Personal Book, deshi. This is extremely important, so memorize it well, deshi!”

“Personal… book?”

Ignoring the confusion on the three adventurers’ faces, Cricket continued the explanation.

“First, hold your hand out in front of you, deshi. Either hand is fine, deshi. But if you don’t feel like it, you don’t have to force yourself, deshi.”

“…Which is it?”

Swift couldn’t help blurting out the obvious contradiction.

Eltz, for his part, felt a quiet admiration for Swift’s courage.

“It looks cooler if you do it, deshi.”

At Cricket’s words, all three extended their hands toward the hearth.

“Yes, just like that, deshi. Now say this: Book—Open!”

“Wait—Book Open?”

The moment Eltz repeated the words, a flash of light burst forth before his outstretched right hand.

“Whoa—what the—?!”

Eltz shouted in surprise, despite himself.

Beside him, Swift stared with his mouth hanging open in equal astonishment.

A loud poof echoed through the room—an almost comically exaggerated sound effect.

And in fact… there really was a small puff of smoke.

Now floating in front of Eltz was a large silver book, hovering in midair.

“That is your Personal Book, deshi,” Cricket explained proudly. “In this world it’s usually abbreviated as PB, deshi. Go ahead and take it and open it.”

Following Cricket’s instructions, Eltz reached forward and opened the book. Swift and Rinse followed suit a moment later.

Inside the book was something unexpected.

Displayed across its pages was what looked remarkably like the desktop screen of a computer.

But that wasn’t the only surprise.

The moment the book opened, a keyboard slid out from between the pages.

“…What is this?” Eltz murmured. “It looks like some kind of computer screen.”

“I’ve never seen this operating system before,” Swift added. “But the interface feels strangely familiar.”

Cricket grinned at their reactions.

“You may think of it as a portable computer, deshi. I’ll explain its functions next, deshi.”

At Cricket’s words, Eltz looked again at the floating display.

A portable computer, Cricket had called it.

But what kind of mechanism could possibly produce such a thing?

After all… the very act of a book appearing out of thin air was something that could never exist in the real world.
