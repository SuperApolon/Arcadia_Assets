Over the past two weeks, the four of them hadn’t merely been idly hunting rock crabs.

They had sold the materials collected from their prey, using the earnings to outfit themselves with weapons and armor, carefully honing the tools that best suited their individual skills.

From the selection of weapons sold in Elm Village, Swift had chosen a spear, inspired by the memory of his duel with Koza. For someone with a keen sense of distance and timing, it was the perfect choice. Lins, recognizing that she was not suited for close combat, had purchased a bow. Though ineffective against the armored crabs, she spent each night in the Elm woods, silently practicing her aim against the trees, alone, perfecting her precision. Chopper, small and agile, had intuitively realized that his strength lay in speed and disruption in close quarters, and he devoted himself to mastering his short blades.

Elz, meanwhile, had acquired every weapon type, carefully analyzing their characteristics and testing their flexibility. He concluded that, against an unknown enemy, a standard longsword—versatile and unencumbered by the weight that slowed axes—was the most practical choice.

With their weapons chosen and honed, the four pressed deeper into the narrowing cave, each step echoing in the confines of the blue-lit cavern. Water splashed around their ankles, and the rhythmic sound of their steps reverberated throughout the tunnel.

――Beyond the narrowing passage, a sudden opening awaits――

Elz recalled Ymir’s warning, his gaze fixed on the darkness stretching beyond the shimmering water. The air had subtly, almost imperceptibly, grown colder.

“The air’s changed,” Swift murmured, tension rippling through the group.

Then, abruptly, the tunnel opened into a larger chamber.

――That’s where Simuluu will be――

Ymir’s words resonated in their minds. This was no mere coincidence—it was an inevitable encounter.

In the dimness above the glimmering blue surface, a shadow moved slowly. Its presence was deliberate, almost sentient, and it seemed to watch the party as they advanced.
