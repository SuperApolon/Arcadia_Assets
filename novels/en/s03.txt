Eltz arrived at the island’s shore still gazing up at the circling birds. What greeted him there was a cliff rising sharply before him, nearly ten meters high.

Though not quite a sheer precipice, the exposed, twisting layers of rock formed a striking work of art—one sculpted by the overwhelming forces of nature itself.

And within that cliff yawned a cave.

The tunnel stretched inward toward the heart of the island, its walls clearly shaped by human hands.

“A touch of human craft mixed into the power of nature… huh. Looks like I’ll be meeting people soon.”

Following the gravel-laid path, Eltz stepped into the cave, guided by the faint glow of lamps fixed along the stone walls.

In the dim half-darkness, he found himself imagining the world waiting beyond.

The encounters he had dreamed of within the realm of VR… the people he would meet.

And yet, a small unease stirred in him.

When he finally spoke to someone in this world for the first time—would it truly be a human?

Then, as every adventurer did when they passed through this tunnel, he emerged into the light.

He raised a hand to shield his eyes from the sudden brightness.

“We’re here… a village.”

Before Eltz’s eyes now spread a peaceful settlement.

Surrounded by flowers and trees, the village seemed almost one with nature itself. Across the open ground stood numerous cone-shaped tents made of straw, some large, some small.

Yet one structure drew his gaze more than the others.

At the far end of the village stood a particularly large thatched building.

Everything here felt new to Eltz.

To be surrounded by such beautiful nature was something impossible in the real world. In a world paved over with asphalt and concrete, one could never taste such pure joy.

He stood there, simply absorbed in wonder.

Perhaps the sight of him—standing still, taking everything in—looked a little amusing. A young man passing through the village on his way toward the cave noticed him and approached with a faint smile.

“Hey there. You a rookie?”

Caught off guard by the sudden greeting, Eltz struggled to hide his surprise. The adventurer watched him with an amused, almost nostalgic expression.

“Uh—yeah. I just logged in today. I figured I’d start by gathering information, so I came here… but I don’t even know where to go.”

“Ah, I see.”

The adventurer nodded, as if remembering his own first day.

“If you’re looking for information, you should start by taking the beginner tutorial at the guild. The guild—see that big building over there?”

He pointed deeper into the village.

“That’s the guild hall.”

“Oh, that building is the guild? Thanks for the help.”

Eltz had noticed it earlier himself—the largest thatched structure in the village.

“Well then, good luck.”

“You’ve been a big help. Thank you.”

The kind young man left without even giving his name.

There was nothing pretentious about it.

It was simply one of those quiet moments of kindness—something commonly seen in the world of MMORPGs.
