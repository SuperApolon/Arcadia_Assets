By the time the training session came to an end, the world around them had been dyed in the deep crimson glow of sunset.

Elts and the others stood before the guild as Cricket and Koza prepared to formally conclude the lesson.

“Honestly… I never imagined I’d be brought to my knees by rookie beginners,” Koza muttered.

“That movement was splendid, deshi. It’s been a long time since I’ve seen something that impressive, deshi,” Cricket said happily, her ears fluttering.

With quick, light steps, she trotted over to them and held out three cards.

“These are your rewards for completing the training, deshi. Go on, take them.”

Each of them received a single card.

The card bore the words “Proof of Beginner Training Completion.”
Illustrated on it was a pendant—crafted from bronze in the shape of a rabbit, its eyes set with small crimson gems.

“This will be the final explanation of the training, deshi.”

At those words, the three looked toward Cricket.

“In this world, items are traded in the form of cards, deshi. Now open your PBs.”

Following her instruction, they opened their Personal Books.

“Turn to the page labeled Inventory, deshi.”

Just as she said, their Personal Books contained several tabbed sections. One of them was indeed labeled Inventory.

When they opened the page, they saw a list titled Item List. Their currently held belongings were displayed there in bullet form, with sorting categories such as weapons, armor, and items.

“So it’s basically an inventory list… this thing really has everything,” Swift said.

The pages that followed resembled a card case. Each page contained four slots shaped like shallow recesses where cards could be placed. Counting them, there were twenty-five pages in total—meaning the book could store up to one hundred cards.

Cricket continued her explanation.

“Only when a card is inserted into one of those pages will it be recognized as an item you possess, deshi.”

Following her instructions, Elts and the others inserted their cards into the Personal Book.

“Now return to the item list, deshi.”

When they did, the item they had just inserted appeared in the list.

Conveniently, the newly added entry was marked with a small “New Item” tag.

“It really did get added,” Elts said in surprise.

“Now that you’ve confirmed it, equip it right away, deshi. There are several ways to equip items, but I’ll show you how to do it from the item list for now.”

There were apparently multiple ways to equip items. Elts silently repeated Cricket’s instructions in his mind so he wouldn’t forget.

“Alright, select the item I just gave you, deshi.”

They did as instructed. Two options appeared.

“Did you select it, deshi? It should ask whether you want to equip it or not. Choose Equip, deshi.”

The moment the three of them selected Equip, a faint white glow appeared around their necks.

From within the soft light emerged the very pendant depicted on the card—a rabbit-shaped charm with crimson gem eyes.

“That’s it. Equipment complete, deshi. Easy, right? And it looks very good on you, deshi.”

The three exchanged glances, examining one another.

Around each of their necks now hung the rabbit pendant, its ruby-like eyes glimmering gently.

It was Cricket’s small gift to commemorate the completion of their training.

Soon after, the three bid farewell to Cricket and Koza.

“Thanks for everything, Cricket. Koza,” Elts said.

Both of them smiled.

“Do your best out there, deshi.”

The three adventurers nodded with smiles of their own.

And so the three set off on their journey.

Cricket and Koza stood watching as the young adventurers descended the gentle slope leading down from the guild. They kept their eyes on them until the three figures disappeared from view.

Once they were gone, Cricket quietly turned and headed back toward the guild.

Then she muttered to herself.

“…Oh no, deshi. I forgot to teach them how to form a party.”

Behind her, Koza collapsed spectacularly onto the ground.

Leaving him there, Cricket walked calmly into the guild.

“These things happen sometimes, deshi. Even the greatest masters make mistakes, deshi.”

Koza slowly pushed himself back to his feet and silently followed her inside.

After all, this too was simply another ordinary day for them.
