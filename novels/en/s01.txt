The sea stretched wide and endless, shimmering like a vast field of blue jewels—clear, translucent, and radiant beneath the morning sun.
Along its edge, a strip of pure white sand gleamed brilliantly in the dawn light.

Together, sea and shore wove a coastline of breathtaking beauty, spreading to the horizon like a grand stage prepared for departure.

This place was known as the Shore of Departure—the point of origin through which every adventurer passed upon arriving in this world.

Now, a single light descended from the sky.

Tracing a gentle arc through the air, the glowing sphere drifted toward the ground. As it landed upon the sand, the light gradually contracted, its brilliance fading and hardening into the dull metallic sheen of iron.

The metal sphere hissed softly, smoke curling from its seams.

Then, with a slow mechanical groan, it began to open.

Something stirred inside.

Within the drifting smoke, a wavering silhouette appeared. At the same time, the faint sound of footsteps pressing into soft sand reached the quiet shore.

From within the sphere emerged a young man with black hair.

“Ugh… it’s smoky. What the hell? So this is how it starts? Kind of sloppy.”

His narrow eyes gazed toward the distant horizon of the sea.

The landscape that greeted him was unmistakably different from the world he knew—the world he had lived in until now.

He took a deep breath.

The air sweeping across the beach was crisp and refreshing.

“So… this is the world of the game.”

Against the pure white sand, his short black hair stood out starkly.
He was of average height, dressed in simple hempen cloth—an outfit befitting a traveler. Here, it was hardly an unusual sight.

The young man’s name was Eltz.

He too was one of the adventurers—players—within this game.

Standing upon the white sand, he pressed his foot into the ground again, as if confirming the reality of the world beneath him.

Crunch… crunch… crunch…

Each step left a clear footprint trailing behind him toward the sea.

“I’d heard rumors, but it really is beautiful… The presence of it is overwhelming—no less vivid than reality itself. So this is what they mean by the ‘presence’ of VRS.”

Before his slightly amber-tinted eyes stretched the vast and majestic ocean.

For an adventurer, the sights one encountered were treasures beyond price.
For Eltz as well, this moment was an irreplaceable experience.

To see with one’s own eyes.
To feel the world with one’s own body.

That was the greatest feature of VRS.

And that experience—this sense of true reality—was exactly what the adventurers who came here sought.

After spending a while admiring the view before him, the inevitable came.

An adventurer must eventually set out on a journey.

Eltz was no exception.

“Well then… what should I do first? The usual approach is to find a town or village. I should probably check what equipment and items I’ve got, too.”

At his waist hung a single copper knife.

Aside from that—and the rough hemp cloth he wore—he had nothing.

“So this is the standard starter gear. Nice weight… the blade’s intact too. Looks usable in a fight.”

He drew the knife and swung it a few times, testing its weight and balance.

It felt lighter and easier to handle than he had expected.

“Combat, huh… I’m looking forward to it. Don’t see any monsters around yet, though. Well, first things first—I should find a town and gather information about this world.”

With that, the traveler reclaimed his true role.

The beginning of a new journey for a lone adventurer.

Upon the white sand, where the waves gently lapped the shore, his footprints remained—clear and unmistakable.
