On the rocky outcrop, they noticed someone already hunting. A small-statured boy was struggling earnestly against a rock crab. For a moment, the group considered moving to another hunting spot, but there were more than enough prey here for everyone.

Elz, feeling a subtle sense of camaraderie with the young, likely novice adventurer, positioned his party a short distance away.

Drawing from the valuable insights gained in their previous battle, Elz reached a conclusion about their next course of action.

“Right now, hunting Lv1 monsters solo is probably more efficient than forming a party to take on stronger enemies,” he reasoned.

“That makes sense. We barely gained any XP last time after all that effort. Plus, solo hunting avoids having to divide items,” Swift agreed.

Meanwhile, Lins looked slightly uneasy. Noticing this, Elz added:

“There’s plenty of prey here. Let’s stick to solo hunting for a while, but don’t stray too far—let’s stay within range to back each other up if anything goes wrong.”

Both nodded, agreeing, and the party was officially disbanded.

Suddenly—a shrill scream pierced the air.

The trio’s eyes snapped to its source. It was the boy who had been hunting solo when they arrived.
