Elts lingered a moment longer, savoring the view from his room, before stepping back into the dim corridor.

“Alright… I’m definitely hungry now,” he muttered after a few steps.

And then, something unusual appeared ahead—a faint, translucent figure.

“What…?” Elts whispered.

The shimmering shape slowly grew clearer.

“Swift?” he called.

But his voice seemed to pass through him. The figure of Swift moved silently toward the inn’s lobby, heading for the counter.

Elts hurried after him, emerging into the main hall.

“Swift!” he called again.

This time, his voice reached him.

“Oh, Elts,” Swift said, finally noticing him. He spoke as though nothing had happened.

“Sorry to keep you waiting. The room was nice. A bit cramped for three people, though. Guess the guys will have to sleep on the floor. You should’ve all come together—it’s got the best view of the guild and the goddess statue from the window. Absolutely gorgeous.”

Elts froze for a moment. Something didn’t add up.

“Wait… I wasn’t waiting. I just came out,” he said.

Swift blinked, confused.

“Huh? Came out? Weren’t you waiting outside? There was only one room. I assumed we’d be sharing.”

At that moment, it clicked for Elts. There was indeed only one door at the end of the corridor. One room. So how could all three of them have ended up there?

As they were puzzling over it, another faint figure materialized in the corridor, slowly approaching them.

“Sorry to keep you both waiting,” came a familiar voice.

It was Rins.

Elts and Swift froze as reality—or at least their perception of it—caught up.

“Wait… what?” Swift muttered, holding his head.

Elts tried to sort through the confusion. They had each taken the corridor to the room. But there was only one room. Logically, all three should have converged there.

And yet—Swift had seen the same view Elts had.

“The guild… and the goddess statue… the view,” Elts murmured.

“So, we each rested in the same room… separately,” he concluded.

It sounded paradoxical, but in a virtual world, such impossible arrangements weren’t entirely unbelievable. Perhaps the corridor acted as a sort of spatial switch, channeling each of them into parallel instances of the same room. That would also explain why their presences had disappeared, and why Swift hadn’t heard him at first.

“Ah… that makes sense. Each of us has our own room,” Elts said, recalling the innkeeper’s instructions.

Swift groaned. “Ugh… my head hurts. So yeah, separate rooms. Makes sense—three of us in one room would’ve been way too tight.”

“Well, no point overthinking it. Let’s head outside. Don’t want to block the corridor,” Elts said.

The three of them continued their conversation as the old innkeeper smiled knowingly.

“The phase is shifted, you see,” she said suddenly, in an almost scholarly tone. “Like when you listen to music with headphones and sense depth. In the world of ARCADIA, we can shift spaces along imaginary vectors to create multiple parallel worlds.”

Elts and Swift froze, uncertain how to respond.

Rins, calm as ever, asked, “Wait… that theorem was only established a few months ago. You’re telling me the developers somehow integrated it into ARCADIA already? That seems… impossible.”

“Young lady, your skepticism is understandable,” the innkeeper chuckled. “But time in this world is infinite. Now, I mustn’t hold you back. For dinner, head to Lemmings Tavern.”

With that cryptic advice, the three set out into the village evening.

Following the scattered lights through the village, they made their way toward the tavern the innkeeper had mentioned. From the guild ahead, past the goddess statue lit by vibrant flowers, they turned toward the forest along a wooden path.

Nestled among the trees, the open-air tavern rose about three meters above ground, surrounded by thick branches forming railings. Hanging lanterns cast a soft glow over the countless adventurers sharing drinks below.

The circular space—twenty-five meters in diameter with 256 seats—was a sanctuary for weary adventurers.

“Let’s grab a table first. I’ll go order once we’re settled,” Swift said.

They chose empty seats and sat.

“Hmm… where do we order? Can’t see any staff,” Swift said, starting to rise.

“Wait—check the menu on the table,” Elts stopped him.

The tavern menu included detailed instructions for beginners:

How to Order at Lemmings Tavern (Beginner-Friendly)

Open your Personal Book (PB).

Select the SHOP-MENU <Menu> icon on your desktop.

Choose your meal and quantity on the displayed screen.

Your food will automatically appear on the central table of your seating area.

Enjoy your meal!

Notes:

Please return dishes to the central table after eating.

Lemmings roam inside to clean—please don’t interfere with their work.

Following the instructions, they opened their Personal Books.

“PB again, huh? This thing gets used a lot,” Elts muttered.

“Yeah… but was there even a SHOP-MENU icon on the desktop?” Rins asked.

A flashing icon popped up as if in response, labeled SHOP-MENU.

“Ah, there it is,” Elts said, clicking it.

The menu appeared:

“Doesn’t really feel like a tavern menu… more like a restaurant,” Elts observed.

Swift and Rins nodded in agreement.

“Everyone’s starting with 100 ELK, right? Feels wasteful to spend too much here,” Swift said.

“No, the inn deducted 10 ELK, so we’re at 90,” Elts said. Checking the PB confirmed it.

“Ah, front payment… must’ve triggered when we signed the ledger,” Swift realized.

They returned to the menu, deciding on their orders. Swift skipped appetizers, opting for corn potage, cheese-steamed Shamellot, and iced café. Elts picked the Scissor Salad, herb-roasted Mumu, and beer. Rins, shyly, joined in on the drinks.

“Let’s start with drinks and toast to our first meeting,” Swift suggested.

“Cheers!” They clinked their mugs.

“Ah… that hits the spot!”

Their laughter filled the tavern.

Swift proposed introductions next.

“Alright, I’ll go first. Player name: Swift. I’ve played plenty of online games, but this one’s by far the best. The immersion is incredible. Meeting you two here… must be fate. So, let’s work together!”

Elts followed: “Player name: Elts. I’ve gamed my whole life, mostly MMORPGs, but I’ve dabbled everywhere—even bullet-hell shooters. Found some fun here after feeling burned out. I may be a bit ridiculous, but… nice to meet you.”

Swift teased him for being a hardcore gamer, and Elts playfully nudged him.

Rins blushed but introduced herself: “I’m Rins. I don’t play much normally, but seeing this world… it’s amazing. I’ll probably slow you down, but… let’s do our best together.”

“Don’t worry, that’s cute,” Swift said without a second thought.

With full glasses in hand, Elts scanned the menu again.

“Maybe I’ll try that Apple Trick… and what’s with the Lemmings mentioned in the notes?”

They glanced over to the small creatures carefully cleaning the tables, their big round eyes and tiny stature endearing.

“Cute,” Rins whispered.

“And that’s why it’s called Lemmings Tavern,” Elts said with a smile.

The warm food, the cheerful drinks, the light chatter—time flew by. Their first night in this new world had begun, marked by the simple joy of newfound friends.
