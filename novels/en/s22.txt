Days passed in a steady rhythm of hunting Chamellots. The time they could devote to hunting each day was limited. Even with a day of rest, four and a half hours was all they could manage. After all, hunting demanded maximum exertion. One target down, and the effort it took was something they all understood more deeply with each passing day.

By now, three days had gone by since they began leveling. Day after day, they wrestled with the rock crabs, yet strangely, no one grew tired of it. In most RPGs, what’s commonly called “level grinding” is a tedious chore for those who aren’t interested. Even among avid gamers, some dislike the grind and resort to cheats or hacks to speed things up.

Elz, however, saw no meaning in such shortcuts. The joy lay in fully enjoying the experience within the game’s limits. Using tools to skip the work and simply showing off one’s progress might feel efficient—and sharing the completed save to receive praise, or posting online to flaunt one’s high level could feel gratifying. Yet Elz placed little value in showing others his achievements. Not that he judged those who did; for busy adults, sometimes cheats are a necessary compromise to reach the ending. What mattered most to Elz was how much he could personally enjoy the game. The focus was on the process, not the outcome. Grinding through thirty-plus rock crabs each day had meaning precisely in how much they could enjoy it.

Fortuitously, even without such a mindset, the hunting system in this world provided adventurers with genuine satisfaction. Elz felt once again the thrill of existing in this game world.

And then, the long-awaited moment arrived.

“Hah!”

With a shout, Elz slashed a fallen Chamellot’s belly with his copper sword. The creature began to disintegrate into particles.

“How many has it been today?”

“I don’t know—I’ve stopped counting,” Swift replied with a laugh.

Then suddenly, Elz’s body was bathed in dazzling light. From his feet to his head, countless luminous particles spiraled upward.

“What is this!?”

By the time he voiced his question, a crisp, satisfying sound effect rang out. The visual spectacle and accompanying noise drew the eyes of everyone around him.

“Could it be…”

A single thought flashed through Elz’s mind:

“Level up!?”

He hurriedly opened his Personal Book to check his stats.

“Congrats!”

His friends rushed over, and Elz smiled, bowing in thanks.

“Thanks,” he said.

“Did your stats go up?” Swift asked.

Elz studied the numbers.

“HP and SP increased, but the other stats haven’t changed. Instead, I got… some kind of ‘points to allocate.’”

“What’s that?”

The others leaned in to peek at his Personal Book.

“Hey, it’s true. What’s that about?”

“I think… if I do this—”

Elz clicked the “Allocate Points” option on the stats screen. Without anyone teaching him, he could intuit that the triangular markers next to each stat were used to distribute the points he had earned.

At the same moment, a critical question took root in his mind: how should he allocate the points? The way these points were distributed could determine his future fortunes. What allocation would be most efficient?

Elz crossed his arms, considering. Once allocated, the points might be permanent—but the previous screen mentioned the possibility of redistribution, giving him some reassurance.

“Hmmm… what should I do?”

Should he spread the points evenly, or concentrate them in one area? The general advantages of each approach were clear. Even distribution would give balanced stats, avoiding critical weaknesses. But balance also meant no standout strengths. In other words, it risked creating a jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none. On the other hand, focusing all points in one stat could unleash explosive power in certain situations. The exact applications weren’t yet clear, but Elz felt confident in that reasoning.

Thus, the choice boiled down to two options. Considering that the targets in the near future would still be Chamellots, the decision became obvious.

Against the Chamellots’ formidable defense, Elz chose a single-minded focus on physical attack power.

Swift muttered quietly, glancing at Elz’s allocation.

“That point distribution… kinda looks like a ‘musclehead’ build.”

“Don’t call me a musclehead! I put a lot of thought into this,” Elz retorted.

Meanwhile, Chopper took a sip from his water bottle and asked,

“What’s a musclehead?”

“It means ‘brains and brawn,’ Chopper. But don’t casually call people that—it can offend them if they don’t realize it themselves.”

Chopper blinked, surprised. The others smiled quietly at the exchange.

With that, they resumed their hunt, chasing after their next level-up with renewed determination.

Under the clear blue sky, Elz and his companions diligently battled the crabs, fully immersed in the joy of their adventure.
