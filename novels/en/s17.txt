After taking breaks and exchanging a few words, Elz and the others continued their hunt. Perhaps due to the intense concentration required—far beyond what was normal—after about an hour and a half, the three were utterly exhausted.

Resigned, they returned to the village and booked a room at the inn.

“All right, let’s have free time until evening. Everyone probably has things they want to do. Meet at Lemmings’ Tavern at 18:00.”

“Why not in front of the goddess statue? It’s easier to find each other there than in a crowded tavern,” Swift suggested.

“Good point. All right, then, 18:00 at the goddess statue,” Elz agreed.

“Roger that!” the others chimed in.

“See you later,” Elz said.

With plans set, everyone headed to their rooms.

Once back in his room, Elz collapsed onto the bed, but the cool sweat on his back jolted him upright.

“I need a shower.”

Muttering “Naked on,” he headed to the washroom, opened his PB, and clicked the appropriate icon, disappearing into the shower room. Warm water splashed over his face as he considered his next steps.

First, he would sell the materials he had gathered at the tool shop. In just an hour and a half, they had hunted over ten rock crabs, gathering a fair number of drop items. Depending on how much money he earned, he might even check the weapon and armor shops. Any remaining time could be spent exploring the northern part of the village, which they hadn’t visited yet.

Exiting the inn, Elz headed straight for the tool shop. It was barely past 15:00. Upon entering, he was greeted by the parrot overhead and immediately opened his PB, clicking the sale icon to bring up the sell form.

Two item types were detected. Elz checked all of today’s acquisitions and entered the quantities.

Inside, countless weapons were displayed: small short swords, slightly longer swords, thrusting spears, single-handed axes with short handles and heavy blades, and elegantly curved bows. All appeared to be copper-made and filled the cases to capacity.

For any RPG player, the weapon and armor shop was a dream come true—something you wished existed in real life. Though such shops could exist in the right places, they were rare in everyday life. Elz’s eyes sparkled as he approached the display.

“Swords, spears, bows… Is that the spear Koza used? So cool.”

Realizing he had been admiring the weapons without opening his PB, Elz hurriedly took it out. But there were only three customers inside, himself included. No need to rush, he thought. He slowly opened the pages and brought up the purchase form.
