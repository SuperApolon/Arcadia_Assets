Swift’s reaction was quick the moment he realized it was loot.

“Elts, you take it,” he said, smiling. Rins nodded in agreement.

Elts knew that trying to decline politely would be disrespectful. With a smile, he thanked them.

After the fight, the trio settled in the shadow near the cliff, opening their Personal Books to rest.

“HP 87… you took quite a beating,” Swift remarked.

Elts glanced at his own stats and froze.

“HP 56…”

“Ha! You pushed yourself too hard. Let’s rest a bit,” Swift laughed.

Then Swift noticed something unusual on his screen.

“Wait… no experience points? Did anyone else get any?”

“Nope, same here,” Elts said.

“Me too,” Rins confirmed.

Swift frowned.

“And it says ‘Not currently in a party.’ How do we even form one?”

“There’s a tab in the status screen labeled ‘Party Formation.’ Maybe that’s it,” Elts suggested. Clicking it revealed the interface.

Elts selected “From communication range,” and the names Swift and Rins appeared.

Clicking ‘Invite’ sent the request. Swift and Rins received it.

“Ah, got an invite,” Swift said.

“There—‘In Party’,” Swift said.

“So that’s how you form a party… why didn’t Cricket tell us?”

They closed their Personal Books. On Elts’ screen, red dots marked monsters nearby.

“Let’s target the Lv1 ones first,” he said. Using Map Scan, they checked the monsters’ levels and prepared to hunt again.

Already practiced from the morning skirmish, the trio worked smoothly. Elts lured a crab from the group with a stone, bringing it within range of Swift and Rins, who flanked it with knives.

“Hey, looks like we can damage it without flipping it this time,” Swift noted.

“True… watch out!” Elts dodged the crab’s claw, striking. A faint glow of life energy rose into the air.

Before Elts could kick it over, Rins’ precise thrust caused the Shamelot to disintegrate into particles.

“Done already? That was fragile,” Swift commented, brushing the drifting particles aside.

Elts considered it.

“Probably, the first one we fought was higher level,” he concluded.

“Oh… makes sense,” Swift murmured, watching the fading particles.

“And no drops this time?”

“Not every fight drops loot,” Elts explained.

Opening their Personal Books, they confirmed no experience points had been gained.

“Do we get XP only when solo?” Swift wondered.

Elts shook his head.

“That would be absurd. If experience only counted solo, this wouldn’t be a true MMORPG. Multiplayer participation is the point. There must be another explanation.”

“Let’s experiment. Solo hunt, then,” Elts proposed.

“Okay, I’ll do the same,” Swift agreed.

They disbanded the party temporarily and each faced a single target alone. Lv1 Shamelots were dispatched one-on-one, power attacks only. After the fight, experience points appeared correctly.

“Solo, we get XP,” Elts said.

Analyzing the patterns, Elts deduced the system:

First fight: Lv3 Shamelot, in a party → little/no XP

Second fight: Lv1 Shamelot, in a party → little XP

Solo fights: XP awarded correctly

Conclusion: experience is divided among party members. To gain meaningful XP in a party, one must fight monsters at or above one’s level. Lv1 solo against Lv3 would yield reasonable XP.

“Got it. Solo is more efficient for now,” Elts decided.

From this brief combat, his keen analytical skills had extracted precise information. The same sharp insight that had served him against Koza now earned the trust of Swift and Rins.

Though brief, the bond between them was solidifying. In this new world, they were beginning to trust each other.
