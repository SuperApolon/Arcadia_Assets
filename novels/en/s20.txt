The clock ticked past 18:00. The sky had begun to glow with the warm colors of sunset.

Arriving fifteen minutes early, Elz stood alone, quietly gazing at the goddess statue. About ten minutes later, Swift and Lince appeared, walking together.

“Hey, Elz, you’re early. How was your free time?”

“Pretty productive,” Elz replied.

Noticing his new equipment, Swift’s voice rose in surprise.

“Your gear’s changed!”

Elz smirked in response.

“We looked too, but it was too expensive, so we skipped it this time,” Swift said.

“If you keep hesitating like that, it’ll always get postponed. Buy it when you feel like it! Use it while you can. I’m down to just 18 ELK anyway.”

“Oi!”

“Anyway, what about dinner?” Swift added with a laugh—just as a voice suddenly called out.

“Um, excuse me.”

The three turned toward the voice. Standing there was a cheerful girl with short, chestnut hair.

“Oh, you’re—”

“Thank you so much for helping my little brother.”

Elz and his friends instinctively bowed in response.

“Oh, your brother? Ah, you’re Chopper’s older sister.”

It seemed she had come to express her gratitude.

“Chopper, make sure you say thank you properly,” she said. Chopper bowed politely.

“No need, really,” he said humbly.

Passing adventurers helping someone in distress was not unusual in this world. Elz and the others hadn’t expected anything in return—they had simply reacted naturally when they saw the fallen boy.

As the group prepared to leave, the girl spoke again.

“So, are you all already part of a community?”

Elz perked up at the question.

“A community…?”

She smiled warmly.

“In this world, there are countless communities—groups established by adventurers. Belonging to one grants access to community-specific facilities, advice from veteran adventurers, and other perks. It’s really convenient.”

“Really? I had no idea something like that existed,” Elz said. The three exchanged glances and murmured their thoughts.

Seeing their hesitation, the girl continued, “Would you like to join ours? I’m Ymir from White Garden. Our community has a large membership, and our top players are quite advanced, so it could be very helpful for you.”

It was a welcome offer for Elz and his friends.

“What do you think?” Swift asked.

“We don’t know much about this world yet. It would be reassuring to belong to a group, but we don’t have enough info to know what impact joining now might have,” Elz replied.

Ymir added reassuringly, “Joining or leaving a community is entirely up to you. You can treat it like a trial. If it doesn’t suit you, leaving is no problem.”

“That sounds good to me,” Swift said. “I’m in favor.”

Elz nodded. “Lince?”

“I’m not sure, but I’ll follow your lead. They don’t seem like bad people,” Lince said.

“Then, if it’s okay, Ymir, we’d like to join,” Elz said.

“Of course! I’ll send the invitation now—just wait a moment!” Ymir’s face lit up as she opened her PB and typed skillfully. Within ten seconds, a message arrived.

■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□■□

Reading the message, Ymir chuckled.

“Oh, don’t worry too much about the wording. Basically, it says: have fun, be friendly, and follow the rules. Our leader’s serious about it, but he’s a good guy.”

“I get it. And wow, he’s hardcore—just reading this, I can tell,” Elz laughed.

“Yes, once you enter Augsbourg, you might be gone a week or two. The invitation says help is available, but the leader himself is totally immersed,” Ymir said, blushing slightly.

“But he’ll probably help when it really matters, right?” Elz asked.

“Yes, of course,” Ymir replied shyly.

“White Garden… that’s a good name. Pure white… garden?” Elz mused.

“Don’t translate it literally!” Swift laughed.

“‘Garden’ at least,” Ymir added with a smile.

“People sometimes call it ‘Hakutei,’ but not often. The leader’s nickname is White Emperor,” she explained, gradually becoming more relaxed in tone. Elz felt a small sense of camaraderie.

After agreeing to the rules, Ymir spoke while looking at her PB.

“Here we go. Click the new community icon on your desktop.”

A new icon labeled White Garden appeared. Selecting it opened the community interface, showing items like Member List and Chat.

“You’ll mainly use Chat. It’s for questions and messages within the community,” Ymir explained. Clicking it revealed a LINE-style interface.

“I’ve introduced you already; the rest should take care of itself,” she added with a laugh, closing her PB.

“Have you had dinner yet?” Ymir asked.

“No, not yet,” Elz replied.

“Then let’s head to the Lemings Tavern together. We’ll have a welcome party later, but for now, let me treat you!” Ymir said, leading the way. Chopper followed eagerly.

Surprised by the sudden invitation, Elz and his friends realized it was better to accept the kindness. Gratitude felt more natural than refusal.

“This is another encounter,” Elz thought. Every meeting like this shaped the world.

“Shall we go?” Exchanging smiles, Elz and his friends followed her, stepping into the soft glow of the tavern where laughter and new bonds awaited.
