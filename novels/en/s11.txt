A soft breeze brushed against his cheeks. Warm sunlight pressed gently behind his eyelids as Elts stirred awake.

Looking around, he found himself in a simple wooden room. Seeing his figure clad in coarse linen, he felt a wave of relief and stretched deeply.

“Good… I’m glad I didn’t wake up in my own room. Would’ve been awful if I’d passed out mid-sleep.”

He made his way to the washroom, splashing cold water across his face.

“Might as well take a proper shower too.”

He remembered the instructions Cricket had given him yesterday about removing clothing. Simply unequipping gear from the item list left the undergarments on; to remove those required a command keyword.

“Naked On.”

Light enveloped his body. But the command wasn’t finalized yet—this was a safety feature to prevent accidental nudity. For ten seconds, a glowing popup appeared in his Personal Book: Unequip (Naked On). Clicking it completed the command; otherwise, it canceled automatically.

After completing the procedure, Elts shed his clothes and stepped into the shower, letting warm water cascade over him.

Morning routines completed, the trio gathered in the inn’s lobby.

“What should we do today?”

“How about we explore outside the village a bit?” Elts suggested. Swift nodded in agreement.

Exiting the inn, they passed through the tunnel they had entered the village by and emerged into the open world. Clear blue skies stretched above, and the vast azure sea glittered in the morning sun.

“Feels like we weren’t even here yesterday. More like… ages ago,” Swift remarked.

“That’s just how packed yesterday was,” Rins replied, smiling.

They strolled toward the coastline, the straight stretch of beach eventually leading back to the starting point of their journey.

“No monsters at the departure beach now,” Elts noted. “Let’s take a tour along the outer coast first.”

The group agreed and set off, cliffs rising sharply on one side, soft sandstone crunching underfoot.

Soon, not far from the Elm Village entrance, jagged rocks jutted from the sand. Around them scuttled dozens of crab-like creatures, about fifty centimeters long.

“What are those?” Rins asked.

“Crabs? Looks tough,” Swift said. Even as they approached, the creatures showed no intention of fleeing.

“They don’t move on their own,” Swift poked one with his knife.

“Non-active monsters,” Elts explained. “They won’t attack unless provoked.”

“Non-active, huh… didn’t think I’d see one in action like this,” Swift muttered, hand on his copper knife.

“So… do we fight it?”

“Could be a good practice. Let’s lure one away and see what happens. Be careful of linking,” Elts added.

Rins looked puzzled. Swift quickly explained.

“In RPGs, non-active monsters don’t attack unless provoked. Active monsters attack on sight. Linking means some monsters help each other—if one is attacked, nearby allies join in. We call that ‘linking.’”

Rins nodded, understanding.

“So this one won’t attack us unless we go near?”

“Exactly,” Elts confirmed with a smile.

“Let’s test it out,” Elts said, pointing to a crab slightly separated from the group.

“You two stay here. I’ll lure it,” he added, moving carefully toward the target. Picking up a small stone, he aimed.

“Take this!”

The pebble hit perfectly. A faint glow of life energy flickered from the crab’s shell. Suddenly, it lunged toward Elts.

“Whoa!”

Swift and Rins watched from a safe distance. The crab charged straight at Elts, who managed to steer it between himself and his teammates.

“Circle around and strike!” he yelled.

The crab struck with its claw, and a small pulse of life energy escaped. Judging from the faint glow, its attack wasn’t too strong. Reassured, the trio brandished their knives.

A sharp snap echoed as their blades struck the shell. The tiny glimmer indicated the monster’s high defense.

“Too tough… barely any damage!” Swift shouted.

“Armor attacks won’t work,” Elts concluded, stepping back. The three formed a loose triangle, cautiously targeting the crab.

“Guess we’ll have to risk a full-strength hit,” Elts said.

“What are you planning?”

Elts grabbed the crab from behind. It thrashed wildly, energy spilling from his body.

“Heavy… and feisty!”

“What are you doing, Elts?!”

“I’m flipping it!”

With a mighty heave, Elts overturned the crab. Its legs flailed helplessly against the ground.

He slashed decisively at its exposed underside. Bright light erupted—his calculations had paid off.

“The underbelly isn’t armored!”

“You… risked yourself just to check that?!”

Elts ignored the comment, continuing his attack. Swift and Rins quickly joined in.

“Finish it before it flips back!” Elts warned, striking again.

The crab’s body glowed brilliantly, then slowly turned translucent before vanishing entirely. A card hovered in the air, shining faintly.

“Did we… defeat it?”

Relieved, the trio examined the floating card. Its nature was immediately clear—it was a reward.
