The white figure glided over the shimmering blue surface, each movement deliberate and regal. Its smooth, pale-gray hide was encrusted in bleached shells, and its long, flowing mane traced the curve of its elongated neck. Majestic antlers rose toward the cavern ceiling from its head, a symbol of its role as guardian of the island.

The luminescent contrast of white against the deep gradation of blue and black left the party momentarily frozen, unable to raise their weapons.

“That… that’s Simuluu,” Elz breathed, his voice barely audible.

None of them had ever seen anything like it. Its very presence was impossible to categorize—an entity both alien and divine. The awe it inspired froze them in place.

Then, the creature’s high-pitched cry shattered the stillness.

“Cuiii—Kyuuiii!!!”

The piercing sound echoed off the walls, a warning to intruders who dared trespass in its domain.

“Oh no… did it notice us?!” Swift shouted, panic flickering in his eyes.

Before anyone could react further, a disturbance appeared on the water’s surface. A translucent sphere, about a meter in diameter, hovered just above Simuluu’s path.

“What… is that?” Elz muttered, staring at the floating water orb.

“It… doesn’t look good,” Swift added, unease creeping into his voice.

The warning proved prescient. Simuluu emitted another sharp cry, and the orb surged forward like a guided projectile.

“Everyone, dodge!!” Elz yelled, drawing his sword. But with Lins and Chopper trailing in the rear, their reactions would inevitably be delayed.

“Damn… too late!”

Elz thrust himself forward, placing his body between the orb and his companions.

“Elz!” Swift shouted.

With all his strength, Elz swung his sword at the orb. The instant it made contact, water exploded outward, a deafening splash reverberating through the cavern.

“Ughh!!”

The impact threw Elz backward toward his comrades, and they all tumbled in a domino effect, sprawling across the slippery, waterlogged floor.

“Elz! You okay?! What the hell are you doing?!”

“Pain… yeah, I’m fine,” Elz groaned, pushing himself upright. The plan to slice the orb in half and neutralize it hadn’t gone as smoothly as imagined.

Swift shook his head in exasperation. “You’re an idiot.”

“I won’t deny it,” Elz replied, grimacing.

“Guys, now is not the time for jokes! Another attack is coming—get ready!” Lins shouted, keeping their focus on the enemy.

Sure enough, Chopper’s warning came just in time: “The orb—again!!”

They turned, and another water sphere hurtled toward them.

“Damn it!” Elz hissed, and this time, the party narrowly evaded it. The orb smashed into the water, erupting into a spray of glistening droplets.

Wiping the spray from their faces, the party quickly reassessed the situation.

“No more distractions. We can’t just stand here admiring Simuluu,” Elz said, determination hardening his voice.

“Leader… orders?” Swift asked.

Elz met the gazes of his companions. “Spread out. Lins, provide rear support from a distance. The three of us will engage up close in a triangle formation. Focus your attacks strategically. Don’t bunch together.”

The plan was simple, yet the stakes were anything but.

Simuluu’s luminous gaze swept across them, water rippling at its every movement. The guardian’s power was clear—every strike, every maneuver would need precision, coordination, and courage.

The first real trial of the cave had begun.
