Kōza served them tea, and once everyone had taken a moment to rest, Cricket suddenly ushered the group outside.

“Before battle, we offer a prayer to the Goddess, deshi.”

“A prayer?” someone echoed.

Led by Cricket, they descended the gentle slope in front of the guild and were guided toward a vibrant flower garden at the center of the village. In the middle of the garden stood a beautiful statue of a goddess, surrounded by blooming colors. Cricket hopped lightly ahead of them, then turned back with a small bounce.

“Take out your PBs, deshi.”

At Cricket’s instruction, everyone produced their Personal Books. Once she confirmed that each of them had done so, Cricket smiled brightly and bowed toward the statue of the goddess. Following her lead, the other three bowed as well.

“Once you’ve finished greeting her, say this: Home Point On, deshi.”

The unfamiliar phrase spread among them as they repeated it aloud.

“Home Point On.”

At that instant, their Personal Books were wrapped in a brief flash of light. The glow lasted no more than a heartbeat before vanishing entirely, leaving Elts and the others blinking in confusion.

“That sets your Home Point, deshi. If a monster defeats you, you’ll return here.”

“So it’s like a Home Crystal in Maters Quest,” Swift remarked.

With that comparison, both he and Elts immediately understood the concept. Not wanting to leave Rins behind in confusion, Swift quickly added,

“Basically, it’s a save point. If you die, you’ll probably respawn here.”

Rins nodded, satisfied.

With the Home Point set, it was finally time to head for the hunting grounds.

“Alright then—let’s go, deshi!”

The group turned on their heels and headed back toward the guild. Passing it by, they climbed a narrow path behind the building. Bright green leaves from low, drooping shrubs covered the trail overhead, forming what looked like a tunnel of foliage.

“Wow… it’s beautiful,” Rins murmured.

Cricket puffed out her chest proudly.

“Of course it is, deshi. The Curtain of Light formed by Euralesian broadleaf trees here on Dolphin Island is famous. Many renowned painters have traveled all the way here just to capture this sight on canvas.”

“You can brag all you like,” Koza muttered nervously, “but why am I being dragged along? At this rate there won’t be anyone left at the guild.”

Cricket answered him with perfect calm.

“The guild can be left to that idiot Rubens, deshi.”

“You’re relying on the guy who’s not even here? That’s the same man you said you’d drown in the River Styx earlier! Honestly, you’re unbelievably irresponsible.”

“If you keep whining, I’ll rip open that coin purse of yours next, deshi.”

“Cute face, filthy mouth,” Koza groaned. “I predict you’re going straight to hell one day.”

Amid the comedic back-and-forth between the two, the group continued onward until they reached their destination.

When they emerged from the green slope, a breathtaking view unfolded before them.

Sunlight filtered through towering broadleaf trees, swaying gently as beams of light danced across the soft grass below.

It was the sort of mystical scene one could never forget.

And there, bouncing across the meadow, was a strange creature none of them had ever seen before. Round, fluffy, and pink, the little creature noticed their presence, curled into a ball, and began rolling across the grass.

“What is that thing?” someone asked.

“Lavi, deshi. That’s your practice target.”

At those words, Elts reached for the bronze knife at his waist. Yet as he began to draw it, he hesitated.

A strange conflict rose within his heart.

“Cricket.”

“Yes, deshi?”

The three of them shared the same thought. In a single moment, they had all reached the same conclusion.

“…I can’t do it,” Elts said quietly. “I can’t kill this monster.”

Swift and Rins nodded silently beside him.

Cricket tilted her head in confusion and leaned forward—just as the three of them spoke in unison.

“It’s too cute!”

Cricket promptly collapsed onto the ground in exaggerated defeat.

Koza, who seemed to have expected exactly this outcome, adjusted his glasses with his middle finger and sighed.

“I told you so. No one with a normal conscience can hunt a Lavi. The only person capable of calmly slaughtering something this adorable is a heartless monster like you.”

Cricket slowly rose from where she lay face-down on the ground.

“…You may be right, deshi. Poor Lavi. Very well. I’ll prepare a substitute.”

“A substitute?”

Elts and the others lowered their bronze knives and looked at her in confusion.

Koza frowned.

“There’s no substitute monster around here.”

Cricket looked straight at him and delivered a single line.

“Koza. You will fight, deshi.”

A moment of silence fell.

“…What?”

Koza’s face twisted in utter confusion before he launched into protest.

“Please don’t joke like that. Why on earth should I fight? NPCs like us aren’t designed for combat in the first place—wait, why are all of you taking battle stances!?”

“…Habit,” Swift admitted.

Koza’s expression darkened.

“I see. So you can’t kill an adorable Lavi, but killing me is perfectly fine.”

“No, that’s not what we—”

Swift’s defense went unheard as Koza’s temper ignited.

“Fine. I accept this challenge.”

At this point, the group had little choice but to go along with it. Besides, as uncomfortable as it was to admit, Koza was still a much easier opponent to strike than the Lavi.

“Change Weapon—Bronze Lance.”

At Koza’s command, a bronze spear materialized in the air and fell neatly into his grasp.

“Don’t expect me to hold back. It’s been a while since I’ve handled a weapon.”

“Then I’ll serve as referee, deshi,” Cricket declared, stepping between them.

“Take your stances, deshi.”

Koza held his spear horizontally at his waist. Elts raised his bronze dagger, seizing the unexpected chance for real combat.

Cricket’s tiny arm lifted slowly into the air—

Then dropped.

“Begin, deshi!”

And just like that, the battle erupted.
