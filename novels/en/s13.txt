After about two hours of exploring, Elz and his group returned to Elm Village for lunch. They had traced the opposite side of the coastline as well, but it quickly turned into rocky terrain, inhabited mostly by Shamelots. By noon, their only loot was a single “Shamelot Shell.”

Seated in Lemings’ tavern, savoring honey jelly, the group discussed their next steps.

“Our information is still way too limited. At this rate, our money’s going to run out soon,” Elz said, and both Swift and Lins nodded in agreement.

“Maybe we can sell the materials we’ve gathered?” Swift suggested.

Elz considered it. “That’s possible. There might be a trading post in the village. But even so… one card like this won’t fetch much.”

“Besides, we haven’t explored the whole village yet,” Swift added.
“Before exploring the island, we should gather info here first,” Lins agreed.

At that moment, a middle-aged adventurer sitting nearby spoke up.

“If you’re looking for the trading post, it’s on the west side of the village. From the entrance, pass the Goddess Statue on your left, and you’ll see three huts. From left to right: weapons, armor, and tools. The tool shop doubles as the trading post.”

Elz and his companions thanked him.

“You’re new, aren’t you? I wasn’t eavesdropping—I just happened to overhear your conversation,” the man said with a reassuring smile.

“No problem, thanks a lot,” Elz replied.
The adventurer nodded in satisfaction.

“Maybe we’ll run into each other again. I’ll give you more tips next time.”

With that, the three left the tavern and walked along a wooden path, past a flower garden in front of the Goddess Statue, toward the huts he had mentioned. Beneath a bright blue sky, three small straw-roofed huts stood in a neat row.

“Rightmost hut, right?” Swift shielded his eyes from the sun.
“Thanks to that man, we saved a lot of time,” Elz said.

Inside the tool shop, a small circular space opened up before them. From the high conical roof, a birdcage swayed, and a vividly colored parrot alternately chirped, “Welcome” and “Irasshaimase.”

Below it, wooden pedestals displayed a variety of items encased in glass.

“Wow, the inside of the shop is like this,” Elz whispered, feeling his heart race at the unfamiliar sight.

Other adventurers glanced at them, smiling warmly.

“I can’t wait to get out of this hemp,” Elz muttered.
“We need to make money first,” Swift said.

Yet there was no shop counter or visible clerk, leaving the group unsure how to buy anything. Their gaze met a female adventurer in magical attire.

“You don’t know how to buy?” she asked.
“Well, actually, we don’t know how to sell materials either,” Swift replied.

The red-haired woman smiled, leaning back as she guided them.

“Open your PB. While you’re in the shop, a communication icon will appear. That’s unfamiliar, right? Click it, and you’ll see ‘Buy’ and ‘Sell.’”

Elz selected the Shamelot Shell to sell, pressed the button, and 25 ELK were added to their funds.

“Sold for 25 ELK!”
A small round of applause broke out nearby.

At that moment, a man with shoulder-length gray hair with black-and-gray streaks approached. He inspected them from head to toe and flipped through his own PB.

“You really just started, huh? Elz, Swift, Lins… all Lv1, I see.”

Suddenly hearing their names, the three froze.

“Surprised I know your names?” he said with a mischievous smile.
The red-haired woman added, “Don’t mind him. You can check basic info in the PB. Use the Viewer, select MapScan—it’ll show player locations, monster levels, and HP.”

Blue dots represent players, red for monsters, and green for NPCs. Clicking on a name shows their level and HP.

“Oh, that’s useful. Thanks, Rose,” Elz said.

The red-haired woman smiled faintly and offered encouragement.

“Mind if I ask one more question? Are there beginner-friendly hunting grounds nearby?” Elz asked.

“Shamelots?” Rose tilted her head. The gray-haired man interrupted: “You mean those crabs.”

“Initially, you might find Lavi easier to hunt,” Rose suggested.
“Lavi? I couldn’t bring myself to hunt them—they’re too cute,” Swift admitted.
“Cute… and therefore unkillable? That’s common,” Rose laughed.

Judah, the gray-haired man, spoke again.

“At Lv1, Shamelots are basically all you can hunt. When you reach Lv3, the Trial Cave is the standard path.”

Elz swallowed his questions, realizing it would be rude to keep interrogating the helpful duo.

“Thank you very much,” he said.
“You’re welcome. Good luck!” Rose replied.

As they left, Judah turned back.

“If it’s the Trial Cave, ask at the guild for info,” he said casually.

Elz paused, taken aback.
“Guess he really is… a strange guy,” Swift said, patting his shoulder and laughing.

Despite the brief interaction, Elz felt a mixture of respect and admiration for the adventurer. Surrounded by this small but growing world, his heart was warmed, and the trio set off once more, ready to continue their journey.
