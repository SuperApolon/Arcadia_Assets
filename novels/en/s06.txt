So I really am inside a virtual world.

Eltz quietly reaffirmed his situation to himself.

“Look at the icon in the upper-left corner of the monitor screen, deshi,” Cricket said.

“The icon in the upper left?”

When Eltz looked where Cricket indicated, he saw three icons there.

“Let’s see… there’s Mail, Messenger, and My Status. Which one?”

“Start with My Status, deshi. Try clicking it.”

Clicking, huh… Without a mouse, that’s a little awkward.

“Is there a mouse or something?”

“There is, deshi. In addition to a mouse, there is also a Click Marker, deshi. With your PB open, try saying: Click Marker—On, deshi.”

A click marker?

The term puzzled Eltz, but he decided to try it anyway.

“Click Marker—On.”

The moment he spoke, a silver pen appeared in midair.

“You can use that, deshi. The mouse works just like normal, so I won’t explain it, deshi. But the Click Marker is more convenient—it also works like a pen tablet if you trace along the screen. Tap the screen once with the tip for a left-click. Tap twice for a double-click. And if you press the button on the back of the pen, that functions as a right-click, deshi.”

“I see. Thanks.”

Following the instructions, Eltz used the Click Marker to double-tap the My Status icon.

A window appeared, displaying a panel filled with information.

“That’s the status screen, deshi. You’ll use it often, so remember it well, deshi. Next icon—the Messenger, deshi.”

The three hurriedly closed the status window and opened Messenger instead.

When it launched, a communication interface appeared—very similar to messaging programs commonly used in the real world.

“The messenger is very convenient, deshi. Using it, you can communicate with friends anytime, anywhere through your PB, deshi.”

“Anytime, anywhere?”

“As long as the other person is able to open their PB, yes—anytime, anywhere, deshi. The only time communication won’t work is if they’re in combat or otherwise unable to check their PB, deshi.”

Cricket bent down slightly and peered into Eltz’s Personal Book.

“Since we’re here, you three should try registering each other as friends, deshi.”

“Oh, good idea,” Swift said.

Eltz nodded as well.

“Let’s see… do I choose this option that says Add Friend?”

“That’s right, deshi. When you select it, adventurers within communication range will automatically appear in a list, deshi. From there, choose the person you want to register as a friend, deshi. Only one person needs to do this step.”

“Then I’ll do it. I want to learn how.”

Swift replied, “Alright, go for it,” and opened his PB.

“By the way,” Cricket added, “the PB has three selectable communication ranges, deshi.”

When Eltz selected Add Friend, just as Cricket had said, a list of player names appeared.

He checked the boxes next to Swift and Rinse and sent the registration request.

“Okay—I think a request just went out to both of you.”

At his words, the two of them looked down at their Personal Books.

“Oh, there it is,” Swift said. “Huh… so that’s how it comes through.”

Swift and Rinse began typing on their keyboards, and shortly afterward Eltz received acceptance notifications from both of them.

“As for email usage, it’s the same as in the real world, so I’ll skip explaining it, deshi,” Cricket said.

Half-listening, Eltz returned his attention to the messenger window.

There, under the Online Friends section, the names Swift and Rinse appeared.

“Oh—your names are showing up here. I guess I just click them?”

Eltz quickly typed out a message and sent it.

Moments later, replies came back from both of them.

“So messaging works just like real life. Being able to use this anytime, anywhere is incredibly convenient. It really is like carrying around a computer. Oh—since separate chats are a hassle, Swift, I’ll invite you into the same message with Rinse.”

“Looks like you’ve already figured out the basics, deshi,” Cricket said with satisfaction. “You learn quickly—it makes me happy, deshi.”

Just then, Koza returned, carrying tea on a round carved wooden tray.

“You’re late, deshi!”

“Well excuse me for having to boil the water first,” Koza replied irritably.

The three adventurers accepted the tea with thanks and took a sip.

“That concludes the explanation of the PB, deshi,” Cricket announced. “Additional functions you can learn yourselves during your adventures, deshi.”

“Thanks for everything, Cricket,” Swift said. “That really helped.”

Cricket blinked in surprise.

“It’s not over yet, deshi.”

“Huh?”

This time, the confused expressions belonged to all three of them.

“After you finish your tea and rest a bit, next comes the combat tutorial, deshi!”

“Combat tutorial?!”

At those words, everyone’s eyes lit up.

Combat—an essential element no adventurer could do without.

Eltz felt his heart pounding with excitement.

With anticipation swelling in his chest, he drained the last of his tea.
