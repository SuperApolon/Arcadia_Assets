Beyond the gently rising wooden path lay the sun-dappled meadow, a place of serene light filtering through the trees. Though Elz had been brought here only yesterday by Cricket and Koza, the memory felt unusually vivid and significant.

As he stood there, mesmerized by the view, a small shadow brushed against his feet.

“Sorry, just passing through,” he said softly, gently moving the little Lavi aside, and began walking along the sunlit sandy path. Surrounded by the soft, almost magical world, he sought the promise of what lay ahead. What might be waiting beyond? Perhaps nothing at all. Yet for an adventurer, the drive to pursue what might exist just beyond one’s reach was instinctive.

The forested space shifted abruptly after a dozen meters. Beyond the meadow stretched a vast expanse of ocean. Considering the island’s size, it was no surprise that the sea was near. A narrow sandy path led to a small pier built from logs. At that moment, a small steamship, billowing plumes of steam, was docking.

“A pier… I can actually board a ship?”

Elz’s heart quickened, and he instinctively broke into a light run. Ships weren’t rare—even in the real world—but here, they carried a meaning all their own. In reality, such sights offered little adventure; at best, a sense of travel. But here, in this world, the thrill of anticipation was tangible. Breathless with expectation, Elz approached the pier.

The gazes of passing adventurers hardly mattered anymore. Looking closer, he noticed many wore the same linen clothing and simple hats as he did.

Wanting a closer look at the ship, he reached for the rainbow-hued archway ahead—but suddenly, light scattered violently, and Elz was knocked backward.

“Ouch… what the—?”

The arch shimmered in all seven colors. An invisible wall of light refracted the scene beyond, and through it, adventurers passed as if nothing blocked them, casting only a glance at the sprawled Elz.

Rising to his feet, he tried again, placing his hand on the arch. Ripples of light radiated from his fingers. Pressing harder, the waves intensified. A solid barrier indeed blocked the way.

“What the heck is this?”

Frustrated, he took a running start to kick at it—but a young adventurer behind him laughed.

“Hey, that’s useless,” the youth said, stopping Elz’s kick.

“You haven’t been baptized, have you?”

“Baptized?” Elz repeated, puzzled.

“This arch is called an Oracle Gate. It’s a kind of sieve, choosing which adventurers can pass. Gates like this exist all over the world, and only those who meet the gate’s conditions can go through.”

“Really? So, what are the conditions for this one?”

The young man watched the docking ship as he spoke.

“You can check by opening your PB in front of the gate. To board the ship here, you need to undergo the Baptism Ritual at the Trial Cave.”

Elz remembered the term from earlier at the item shop.

“You can accept the Baptism Quest at the guild. But with your current level, it’s a little early. Aim for around Level 3, then give it a try.”

With that, the adventurer left. Friendly? Unfriendly? He had simply delivered the information, then hurried aboard the ship.

So, what Elz had learned: to proceed further, he needed to complete this Baptism Quest at the Trial Cave. Additionally, his current level was too low to attempt it safely.

Elz brushed the small wood chips off his clothes and stared at the docked ship.

“Guess I’m on hold for now… I’ll come back later.”

As the ship blew its steam whistle and set off, Elz quietly turned his back, a mixture of hope and determination swelling in his chest.
