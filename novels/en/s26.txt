The entrance to the cave, surrounded by jagged reefs, was partially flooded with seawater. The group carefully stepped onto the narrow ledges along the walls to avoid the water and entered the cavern. As they crossed the threshold, the cave’s chill enveloped them, and they instinctively paused.

“What… is this?”

Elz’s question echoed softly. It wasn’t that the sudden change in air temperature caught them off guard—no one could have noticed it anyway—but the words lingered in the quiet.

“This… can’t be real,” Swift muttered, and no one followed with another comment. His words alone spoke volumes.

Before them spread a scene so mesmerizing that they could do nothing but stand frozen. The flooded water glimmered in deep azure, radiating a blue glow that bathed the entire cave. The pure, untainted color was more beautiful—and more sacred—than anything they had ever seen. Words failed them, drowned by awe.

Even before they had arrived, no one could have prepared them for the information this visual alone would impart. For adventurers visiting for the first time, explaining the cave’s nature was a forbidden act—a silent rule understood by those who had already undergone the baptism. The subtle gestures of care from Ymir and Koza now revealed themselves as a priceless gift to Elz and his companions. As the moment stretched on, their initial confusion slowly transformed into pure, unadulterated wonder.

“I’ve never seen anything this beautiful. That grassy clearing with the sunlight streaming through the trees was lovely, but… this… this is on a whole other level,” Swift whispered.

He stepped closer to the water’s edge, scooping up a handful of the glowing blue liquid. Droplets slipped through his fingers, twirling in the air before merging back into the surface. The group watched in complete silence, mesmerized.

Every adventurer who stepped into this cave for the first time lost their words. Every one of them carried the memory of this breathtaking scene behind their eyelids as they continued their journey.

Over time, the cave became known among adventurers by two names. One was the official Trial Cavern, and the other…

The Blue Cave.

That name spread quickly.

Time seemed to pass unnoticed as the party lingered, eyes still fixed on the scene that would never fade from memory.

“Let’s go… further in,” Elz finally said, recalling Ymir’s warning from before they entered:

――Make sure to enter the cave as a party. Otherwise, you’ll end up fighting alone――

This was the Battlefield (BF), as Ymir had called this place.

――Once inside, head toward the innermost depths――

The luminous blue water led onward. The path, about one and a half meters wide, carried the four adventurers in two columns: Elz and Swift in front, while Lins tightly held Chopper’s hand in the rear.

The cave’s limestone walls were dotted with lamps, subtly human-made, offering reassurance that this place was not entirely abandoned. Guided by the soft white light reflected in the deep blue, they pressed forward, step by step, deeper into the heart of the cave.
