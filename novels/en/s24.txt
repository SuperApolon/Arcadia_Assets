The island, commonly called Dolphin Island, had no real name of its own. On it lay a cave known as the Trial Cavern. A wooden path stretched from the guild’s back entrance along the western coastline, leading northward to where the cavern yawned open, awaiting adventurers.

Two weeks had passed since Elz and his companions began their relentless hunt of rock crabs. By now, all of them had reached Lv3. After two weeks of continuous hunting, each of them had begun to move differently from ordinary people when confronting the crabs. The crabs only had three types of claw attacks—forward jab, upward swing, and downward strike—and the moment when they swung upward was the perfect chance to knock them over. Understanding this and actually executing it was no small feat, but at least for Elz and Swift, their bodies had learned to react instinctively.

The two had reached Lv3 four days ago. Since then, they had waited for Lins and Chopper to catch up so the party could move forward together.

“Finally,” Elz muttered.

Led by Ymir to the guild, the group was met by Koza and an unfamiliar boy.

“Hey, long time no see. How’ve you been, Koza?” Ymir greeted cheerfully.

“Ah, everyone, Ymir seems as energetic as ever. And you, Elz, how have you been? What brings you here today?”

“We’ve been doing well, thanks. By the way… where’s Cricket today?” Elz asked.

Koza frowned slightly.

“That strange little creature isn’t here right now.”

“Strange creature?” Swift laughed. “What happened, is everything okay?”

Koza looked off into the distance, replying softly.

“Two days ago, Desi went off on a journey, and that’s the last we’ve seen of him. Honestly, I’d be relieved if he just disappeared entirely.”

The group couldn’t help but chuckle. Whether it was entirely true or not, given Cricket’s personality, it wasn’t hard to believe.

“Koza-san, you shouldn’t be feeding everyone strange stories,” said the unfamiliar boy.

With gentle fox-like eyes, he corrected Koza.

“Cricket-san is just on paid leave right now.”

“Paid leave?”

Koza fell backward dramatically, flabbergasted, while the rest of the group exchanged bewildered looks.

“Rubens, come here for a moment.”

Adjusting his slightly crooked glasses, Koza beckoned the boy, Rubens, over. He dragged him a short distance away from the others, speaking in low tones. Their conversation carried clearly to Elz’s ears.

“Rubens, you haven’t forgotten the cardinal rule of this job, have you?”

“Cardinal rule?” the boy asked, voice calm and gentle.

Koza’s face clouded with exasperation.

“The cardinal rule is that you absolutely cannot leak certain information to the people experiencing this world. Remember, this is a game world. You must never reveal real-world knowledge to the players—it would ruin their immersion. Take, for example, your ‘paid leave’ comment—absurd, right? That much you should understand.”

“I see… got it,” Rubens replied, though it was hard to tell if he really understood.

Koza led him back to the group.

“Sorry about that. It’s nothing you need to worry about,” Koza said, brushing it off.

Rubens smiled at the group.

“Sorry everyone. I just started this job, so I’m still figuring things out. But the pay’s good, so I need to work hard… it’s tricky!”

“Pay?”

Elz and the others repeated, and Koza went into another exaggerated tumble, surrendering to Rubens’ naïveté. After that, he let the boy be and returned to business.

“So, back to the matter at hand. Why are you all here?”

“Oh, we want to go to the Trial Cavern,” Elz replied.

Koza’s gaze sharpened for a moment, a fleeting look from a past battle that made the group pause briefly.

“Ah, already at that stage, huh? Two weeks of work… seems about right,” he said, closing his eyes as if recalling a distant memory. Then he opened them again, resolute.

“Follow me. This way.”

Leading them toward the guild’s back entrance, Koza began explaining.

“The guild in this world has another side to it,” he said.

“Another side?” Elz asked.

Koza nodded quietly.

“Originally, the guild’s purpose here is to assign adventurers trial quests. In this village, it just so happens the guild has taken on an additional role. From now on, whenever you go to the guild, always remember to open your PB first.”

Elz and the others nodded.

“Your journey has only just begun. I pray that your path shines brightly,” Koza said, gently placing his hand on the door.

“May light guide your journey. Good fortune to you.”

As he spoke, light spilled from the door. Through the radiance, they glimpsed lush greenery and the blue sky beyond.

Walking along the wooden path toward the shore, Elz reflected on Koza’s words.

――This path is only for those who undergo the baptism.――

Ymir, already having received the baptism, was held back by Koza at the guild.

Through the path, the ocean came into view.

――Be careful. The BOSS can be defeated if everyone cooperates.――

Ymir’s words lingered in their ears. She had said this at their parting.

With Ymir staying behind, Chopper could not hide his unease.

“Don’t worry, Chopper. No need to be so anxious. Games are made so that every challenge can be overcome.”

“Enjoying the situation you’re given… just like Elz always says… but I’m still nervous,” Swift admitted.

Before them, a shadowy cave mouth opened upon a wave-lashed reef.

Their first trial quest awaited. Could they overcome it? No—they must. Trials only hold value when conquered. Elz recalled the words of the poet Lucas.

“Let’s go, everyone.”

They nodded in unison.

And, guided by the sound of the waves, they stepped quietly into the darkness of the cavern.
