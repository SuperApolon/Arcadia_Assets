At Elts’s command, the four sets of footprints scattered, sending up a spray of water in all directions. Closing the distance to Simuluu, Elts began analyzing this mysterious water sphere attack. For now, he decided to put aside the question of how the water spheres floated in midair despite not being in zero gravity.

From what he had observed so far, the spheres followed a straight trajectory from their origin to their target. If that was the case, the method for dodging them was perfectly clear: approach Simuluu with lateral, not vertical, movement. As long as one stayed out of the water sphere’s straight path, they could avoid being hit. There was a slight delay from the moment the sphere formed to when it reached its target, which was more than enough time to dodge.

"Everyone, while the sphere is visible, move diagonally relative to Simuluu," Elts instructed.

Of course, this applied when they were at a distance. In close combat, that distance shrinks nearly to zero. Could they still dodge the sphere then?

The third sphere was launched at Rinse, who had been attacking from afar with her bow, likely making her the target. Following Elts’s analysis, Rinse simply sidestepped, avoiding the attack with ease. From there, Simuluu lowered its head into the water once again, beginning to form another sphere.

At that moment, Elts realized something important: the sphere wasn’t rapid-fire. In other words, it probably couldn’t be shot in quick succession. It took at least ten seconds to form a single sphere, meaning that even at close range, seeing the initial movement was enough to dodge it.

Understanding this, Elts rushed toward the target and was once again struck by the sheer size of the creature. But there was no time to hesitate. He drove his sword into the massive three-meter-long body. The glow of life energy emanating from the creature confirmed the damage. Seeing that glow, Swift and Chopper, who had been hesitant to approach, gathered their courage and joined in.

As long as they avoided the spheres, there was no problem. This slow-moving creature was an ideal target up close. Moving around Simuluu while striking, Elts took a position near the long tail at the rear.

In a brief lapse of caution, the whip-like tail struck Elts squarely in the face, and he collapsed to the ground.

"Elts! Are you okay!?"

Seeing him fall, Swift and Chopper, attacking from the sides, froze in confusion.

"Don’t attack from the rear, you two. This is exactly what happens," Elts said, and both of them swallowed nervously and nodded.

The key points became clear. Beyond dodging the spheres, they had to watch out for Simuluu’s whip-like tail, which struck within a radius of 1.5 meters and about 120 degrees to the rear. From his previous analysis, the creature’s forward vision was probably less than 120 degrees. That left a critical 120-degree blind spot—likely 60 degrees to each side. Swift and Chopper, attacking from the sides, faced no reaction from Simuluu, simply receiving damage without retaliation. Since Rinse was constantly targeting it from the front with her arrows, it seemed the monster only attacked adventurers it had in its line of sight.

Acting on this deduction, Elts circled to the side where Chopper was positioned.

"Chopper, never go in front of or behind Simuluu. Always attack from the side," he instructed. Chopper nodded, and the party renewed their coordinated assault.

As Elts predicted, Simuluu, now attacked from the front and both sides, continuously launched spheres at Rinse in the front.

But sadly, having anticipated this attack pattern, it was only a matter of time before Simuluu fell. A massive wave of life energy erupted, scattering through the cave in a display both beautiful and fleeting.

Within this stunning cave, the great guardian of the island quietly gave way. Elts and his companions paid their respects to this majestic being, drawing strength from the battle itself. This was a scene that had repeated countless times between adventurers and mighty creatures; overcoming such a magnificent existence was, in truth, the very form of the “baptism” that this trial represented.

Four silhouettes moved gracefully in the Blue Cave. Before the fallen guardian on the water’s surface, stood those who had just overcome the trial.
