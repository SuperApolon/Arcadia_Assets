From the Shore of Departure, a single stretch of coastline wound its way between scattered islands like a narrow thread across the sea. At the far end of that shore lay the small island travelers sought.

This unnamed island, belonging to the Timnail Archipelago, was commonly called Dolphin Island. True to its name, its curved shape resembled the arched back of a dolphin leaping above the waves.

The island was lush with greenery. Blessed with a warm climate, it had become a paradise for birds and beasts alike. Fierce monsters were nowhere to be found here; only low-level creatures roamed the land. For that reason, it was considered the perfect place for novice adventurers to learn the fundamentals of the hunt.

And yet, there was another important reason travelers set their sights on this island.

On the southern edge of the island—barely two hundred meters across at its widest point—stood a small village nestled within the green.

It was the village of Elm, likely the first place most adventurers would ever visit.

Here, newcomers would learn the rules of this world… before setting out on their journeys beyond it.

In truth, there was only one overland path leading away from the Shore of Departure—the very coastline that led straight to this island. Once Eltz realized that fact, the uncertainty that had lingered over his destination faded, replaced by quiet certainty.

Judging by the position of the sun, he estimated it would take about an hour to reach it.

Before long, the pure white sand beneath his feet gave way to coarse gravel.

Perhaps because of the sea so close by, birds wheeled overhead—creatures resembling seagulls, their white wings speckled with black. Their cries echoed across the shoreline as they glided through the wind.

“I’ve never seen birds like those before… I wonder what they’re called. Are they monsters too?”
