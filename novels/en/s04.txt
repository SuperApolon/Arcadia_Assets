Feeling the gentle sunlight filtering through the thick canopy of trees, Eltz made his way toward the building the kind adventurer had pointed out earlier.

Throughout the village he could see figures who were clearly adventurers. Some wore solemn suits of armor; others draped themselves in leather cloaks and the pointed witch hats often associated with mages. Compared to them, Eltz—dressed only in plain hemp cloth—felt conspicuously under-equipped. In fact, he seemed to be the only one dressed so simply.

As he climbed the gentle slope leading up to the guild, he felt a faint embarrassment under the occasional glances of passersby. Still, he hurried on toward his destination.

At the top of the slope, the building that had seemed distant moments ago now loomed directly before him.

“Up close… it’s even bigger than I expected.”

Judging by its scale, it would probably have been the equivalent of a three-story building in the real world. Yet astonishingly, the entire structure was made of wood. Despite its size, every part of it appeared to be constructed from timber and thatch alone—there were no visible metal fastenings anywhere.

How such a structure had been built was beyond Eltz’s imagination. But one thing was certain: it was the work of remarkable craftsmanship.

That alone was enough to captivate him.

Like a country bumpkin visiting a great city for the first time, he caught himself staring in awe. Shaking himself back to his senses, Eltz stepped through the entrance of the guild.

Inside, a spacious hall opened before him.

The high ceiling was supported by a lattice of wooden beams. From them hung animal hides—likely trophies from hunting—being slowly cured by the smoke rising from a central hearth.

Near that hearth, two adventurers were relaxing.

Standing just inside the entrance and glancing around uncertainly, Eltz quickly realized the attention he was drawing.

“Hmm… I wonder if this is the right place…”

He muttered the words to himself, unsure what to do next.

Just then, one of the young men sitting by the hearth rose to his feet.

“What’s up?”

Perhaps it was the light of the hearth smoke, but the young man’s shoulder-length hair seemed to gleam with a silver hue.

“Um, excuse me. I heard they’re doing beginner lessons here…”

“Beginner lessons? Ah, I see. So you’re a rookie too.”

The word too made Eltz take another look at the young man. Like himself, he wore nothing more than simple hemp cloth.

“Wait—so you’re a beginner as well?”

Eltz blurted the question without thinking.

The young man nodded.

“Yeah. Apparently there’s some trouble nearby, so the guild staff are all out dealing with it. Go ahead—have a seat.”

Following the invitation, Eltz sat down beside him.

“I’m Swift. As you can see, I just started today too. Oh—and she’s a beginner as well.”

At Swift’s words, Eltz turned his gaze toward the girl sitting across the hearth.

The firelight reflected off her long hair, which shimmered like spun gold. When her pale blue eyes met his, Eltz momentarily forgot how to speak.

“I met Rinse here today as well. Hey—what are you staring at, man?”

Swift jabbed him teasingly in the side.

Flustered, Eltz quickly introduced himself.

“I’m Eltz. Nice to meet you.”

The girl called Rinse—whose quiet presence carried a faint air of mystery—gave a small nod in silent greeting.

After their brief introductions, Swift immediately began peppering Eltz with questions.

“So, Eltz—what kind of games do you usually play?”

“I try a bit of everything, but mostly RPGs.”

“RPGs, huh. Do you prefer single-player or MMOs?”

“I like both… but if I had to choose, probably MMOs.”

“Huh. Why’s that?”

The question caught Eltz slightly off guard. No one had ever asked him so directly why he liked MMORPGs.

“Why… well, that’s actually a hard question when you put it like that. What I’m about to say is just my personal opinion.”

As he spoke, Eltz quietly organized his thoughts.

MMORPGs often contained many different concepts within them—speedrunning, character growth, obtaining the strongest equipment, exploring the world’s lore, or simply enjoying conversation within communities like guilds.

Different players sought different things.

But the greatest value he personally felt from MMOs was something else.

“Because you can feel the presence of other people.”

Swift, who had been stretching lazily, suddenly stopped.

“Hm? What do you mean by that? Like competing with other players? If not… it kind of sounds a little lonely.”

“Maybe closer to the latter,” Eltz admitted. “Like… a second life. I sometimes think it would be nice if we could live in a society closer to our ideals than reality.”

Swift could only respond with a wry smile.

“You’re a strange guy, Eltz. Well, whatever. Let’s change the topic—tell me what games you’ve played.”

“Things like Masters Quest… and Alpha Rose.”

“Oh, you played MQ? Seriously? What nation did you start in? How far did you get?”

“Carnail. I cleared the final trial, reached max level after the last limit break, defeated the Thirteen Dragons, and fully upgraded my relic.”

Swift practically jumped to his feet.

“Are you serious!? That’s hardcore!”

“Is it?”

“That’s absolutely hardcore!”

Their conversation had quickly turned into an inside joke that only players of Masters Quest would understand. Swift quickly glanced at Rinse and added an explanation.

“Sorry, Rinse. We’re talking about an older online game. But seriously, Eltz—you’re a total hardcore player. I’m impressed. So what about this game? Are you planning to master it too?”

“Hmm… maybe, if it suits me. To be honest, this is my first experience with VRS. I’m not sure yet.”

At present, ARCADIA was the only game in the world equipped with a full VRS system. Naturally, every adventurer here was experiencing true virtual reality for the first time.

Being skilled at conquering games in the real world didn’t necessarily guarantee success here.

In this world—where all five senses were fully engaged—something that traditional games rarely emphasized became strikingly clear:

human nature.

Even in conventional online games, personality played a role to some degree. But those worlds remained closed spaces protected by anonymity.

Here, in virtual reality, every action revealed its true nature.

Every gesture, every choice drew subtle reactions from the people around you—tiny shifts in expression that your senses would perceive as real experience.

Amid their lighthearted conversation, Eltz felt both the wonder of virtual reality…

and its danger.

This place was a society of its own.

Yes—this was not merely a game.

Still, there was no reason to become overly serious about it.

For now, Eltz decided, the best thing to do was simply enjoy the world he had been given.

And so he remained there, letting himself be carried along by the conversation.
