As faint light began to spill from the Lemmings Tavern into the darkness of night, the adventurers raised their cups beneath the glow of the vine-hung lanterns.

"Then, to celebrate successfully clearing the Trial Cave—cheers!"

At Ymir’s shout, everyone echoed in unison.

As Chopper spilled a generous amount of apple juice over himself in the excitement of the toast, Ymir laughed while helping wipe his clothes and prompted the group for their impressions of the first trial.

"So, how was the first trial? Any thoughts?"

"Piece of cake! Right, Chopper?"

Chopper nodded with a simple “Yeah,” and as he lowered his hand, he spilled the rest of the apple juice. Ymir, on the verge of exasperation, snapped, "Hey, clean it up yourself!"—but Elts just waved it off and ordered a second mug of beer.

"Too fast, man," Swift muttered, but Elts ignored him, raising the second mug with a self-satisfied grin.

"Humans are creatures who want to drown themselves in something, Swift," he said softly.

"What’s he on about now?" Swift muttered.

Unfazed, Elts continued, "It’s a truth born of human weakness. So, what value is there in resisting it?"

Elts was about to give an answer to that very question.

"Representing all weak humans, I hereby declare…"

He raised his voice proudly.

"One more round!"

Holding the empty second mug aloft, he thrust it across the table. Swift rapped Elts on the head with a satisfying thwack.

"But I have to admit, when that water sphere thing appeared, I got scared," Swift said.

"Water sphere? Ah, the Water Sphere. Yeah, seeing it for the first time is shocking," Ymir nodded in agreement.

"That’s its name? Is it some kind of special move?"

"It’s magic," Ymir replied, and the party’s hands froze mid-toast.

"…Magic?"

"Yeah. You don’t see it much on this island," Elts muttered seriously.

"Magic… like that magic?"

"Which magic do you mean? Are you drunk, Elts?" Swift shot back wittily, prompting Elts to mutter repeatedly, "Magic… magic?"

"In the continent you’ll soon head to—Lexia—it’s a common sight. You’ll probably be amazed the first time you see it," Ymir added.

"Lexia, huh… can’t wait," Swift said, his gaze distant with longing.

"Ah, excuse me for a moment," Ymir said, standing up.

"Bathroom?" Elts asked.

"Wait, what? Asking a lady ‘bathroom?’ is totally unacceptable!" Ymir protested, laughing.

"Not the bathroom?"

"No! I just have a personal matter to attend to, and I thought opening the PB in front of everyone would be inappropriate," she explained.

Satisfied, Elts returned to his mug.

After Ymir left, Elts spoke solemnly.

"Everyone, we may have just realized something incredible."

"What is it?" Rinse asked.

Elts paused for a moment, then spoke quietly.

"What do you think is humanity’s strongest desire?"

"What’s that supposed to mean? If it’s something stupid, I’ll be mad," Swift said.

"No, Swift. Listen seriously. What do you think it is?"

Swift and Rinse exchanged glances, then spoke their thoughts.

"Hmm… I guess hunger? You have to eat to survive."
"Sleep, maybe?" Rinse added.
"There’s also sexual desire, but you can live without that. Hunger and sleep are essential. It has to be one of those two," Swift concluded.

Elts nodded slowly, acknowledging their reasoning.

"True, those two are important. But isn’t there something even more important?"

Swift and Rinse exchanged uncertain glances, unable to come up with another answer.

Elts smiled and said,

"The strongest desire… is the urge to relieve yourself."

Swift’s mug raised sharply as he shot back,

"You’re telling a poop joke after dragging us along like this? Come on, drunkard!"

The group laughed. Chopper didn’t fully understand, but he laughed at Elts getting tapped on the head.

"No, seriously. Think about it. Even if you’re starving, exhausted, or your comrades are in mortal danger, if you’re about to lose control, you can’t resist, right?"

"Wait a sec. That’s not really a desire," Swift laughed, trying to restrain Elts.

"We were supposed to finish today with a perfect day—witnessing surreal sights, battling a mystical creature—but why end with a poop joke? What’s your point?"

Elts looked at them, calm, and said simply,

"In other words… there are no toilets in this world."

The group froze for a moment. Indeed, since arriving, none of them had felt the need. They had eaten, but the act of excretion had never even crossed their minds.

"You eat, but nothing comes out. In this world, I think the concept of excretion has simply been cut," Elts explained. Slowly, his words began to make sense to them—it was a blind spot they had never considered.

Elts smirked as if to say, Gotcha. Swift muttered,

"But isn’t this still a poop joke?"

"I won’t deny it," Elts replied.

The table echoed again with the sound of light-hearted teasing.

And so, the adventurers enjoyed trivial conversation, their eyes already drifting toward the new continent they would soon explore. Having faced the trial on this nameless, small island, their hearts had already set sail for the next land. The magic Ymir had spoken of, the various regions awaiting them, the monsters that roamed… new equipment to don.

Their journey would begin tomorrow, and from there, a whole new world would unfold.

With the excitement of a new world as the perfect accompaniment to their drinks, the adventurers continued to talk and laugh late into the night.
